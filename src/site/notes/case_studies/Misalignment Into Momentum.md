## The Challenge

A development team I was working with was struggling to *deliver what was expected from their management and stakeholders*. Leadership saw the team as **lacking initiative**, while the team itself felt they were **missing clear opportunities** and were unsure what was truly expected of them.

This disconnect led to frustration on both sides—**deadlines were missed, trust eroded, and micromanagement increased.**

## My Approach

To bridge this gap, I implemented **a structured Sprint Review process** where:

- The conversations focused on **outcomes** rather than **output**.
- The **team could showcase their work**, proving they were delivering on their commitments.
- **Stakeholders had dedicated time to clarify priorities**, ensuring everyone was aligned on upcoming needs.
- The **team gained autonomy**, eliminating the need for micromanagement and freeing leadership to focus on higher-value work.

## The Results

- **Stakeholders saw real progress on the outcomes that were important to them** and felt more confident in the team’s work.
- **The team gained clarity**, reducing stress thus improving engagement and quality.
- **Leadership stopped micromanaging**, allowing both the team and management to work more efficiently.

By **creating alignment and structured dialogue**, I helped transform a **struggling, misaligned team into a confident, value-driven unit**.
